Stephen Hawking

The man who loved innovation


Stephen William Hawking CH CBE FRS FRSA was an English theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author.


Here's a time-line of Stephen Hawking life:


  • 1942 - January 8, 1942: Stephen Hawking was born in Oxford, England.

  • 1959 Hawking began his undergraduate studies in physics at University College, Oxford.

  • 1962 - He received his Ph.D. in cosmology from Trinity College, Cambridge.

  • 1963 - Hawking was diagnosed with a rare form of motor neuron disease, which eventually left him completely paralyzed.

  • 1965 - Hawking married Jane Wilde, whom he had met while studying at Cambridge.

  • 1974 - Hawking's research into black holes and quantum gravity led him to propose the theory of Hawking radiation, which suggests that black holes emit radiation and eventually evaporate.

  • 1985: - He published "A Brief History of Time," which became a best-seller and made him a household name.

  • 1988: - Hawking's first marriage to Jane Wilde ended in divorce.

  • 1995 - Hawking married Elaine Mason, his former nurse, but the marriage also ended in divorce in 2006.

  • 2004 - Hawking announced that he had solved the black hole information paradox, a long-standing problem in physics.

  • 2014: - The film "The Theory of Everything," which tells the story of Hawking's life and work, was released.

  • 2018 -March 14, 2018: Stephen Hawking passed away at the age of 76 in Cambridge, England.

Hawking was awarded numerous honors during his lifetime, including the Copley Medal, the Albert Einstein Medal, and the Presidential Medal of Freedom. He was also a fellow of the Royal Society and a member of the Pontifical Academy of Sciences.
